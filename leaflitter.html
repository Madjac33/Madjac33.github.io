On the forest floors of the Delaware Water Gap, dead leaves and rotten logs are the homes of many different animals. Some reptiles that inhabit rotten logs are the five-lined skink and the northern fence lizard.

A diverse array of critters find safety in leaf litter. These include mice, shrews, earthworms, beetles, and millipedes.
